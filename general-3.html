UX / UI Project

In 2021 I joined a UX/UI course with ALLWOMEN in Barcelona. Below I have summarised my work for my final project. I was interested in the following problem:

“I don’t write enough. I know it is good for me, I know it would improve my life, but I still don’t write enough.”

Once I chose a problem I was interested in solving, I set out to solve it. Below you can scroll me my research and design process to learn more!

Intro.

Before beginning the research, the problem needed to be fully defined, and for that to happen the problem needed to be pinpointed with more clarity and precision.

I started with jotting down the perceived problem, then writing an initial hypothesis and Problem Statement.

The 5 Whys.

To go further, the problem needed to be fully explored, and so I used the ‘5 Whys’ technique to tease out the root problem. This involved answering the question ‘Why?’ consistently until I got to the real problem.

Why?

Because different people have different reasons to write and different ways of organising their thoughts at different times.

Writing
it all down

I wrote down all the assumptions that I have made from the previous brainstorming techniques, and then listed the thing I wanted to learn about writing.

Conducting the Research

I wrote down all the research questions I wanted answered, then worked out a strategies to accurately attain this data.

Interviews

I set up interviews with 5 people, and asked them the same questions, to find out how they feel about writing and writing things down.

Interview results

Below I grouped all the quotes and ideas that came out of the interviews. I clustered them into the relevant themes that stood out. I started seeing some trends and connections, and drew results to prove or disprove my earlier assumptions.

Survey Results

I generated data from the results to further extract patterns and trends out of the data.

Revisiting the Assumptions

I went back to my original hypothesis and assumptions, and checked off anything that turned out to be true.

Who are these writers?

Out of the results I found 3 types of writers (users).

The concept

After better understanding the users and grouping the users into 3 main personas, I started looking at the user journey. I summarised my findings in this flowchart. Then I excitedly started sketching some concept ideas.
